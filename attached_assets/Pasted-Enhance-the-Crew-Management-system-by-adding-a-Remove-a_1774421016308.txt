Enhance the Crew Management system by adding a "Remove" action and "Re-activate" option.

Do NOT break existing functionality. Extend current system safely.

---

1. ADD "REMOVE" BUTTON (UI)

---

In the Actions column:

Final order:
View | Reject | Blacklist | Remove

* Add "Remove" button after Blacklist
* Style:

  * Subtle red/danger tone
  * Optional trash icon
  * Same spacing & alignment as other actions

---

2. REMOVE CONFIRMATION

---

On clicking "Remove":

Show popup:
"Are you sure you want to remove this crew member?
They will no longer be able to log in."

Buttons:

* Confirm Remove
* Cancel

---

3. BACKEND LOGIC (SAFE APPROACH)

---

Do NOT delete data.

* Add new status: "Removed"
* When removed:

  * status = "Removed"
  * Keep all historical data (events, payments, attendance)

---

4. LOGIN RESTRICTION

---

If removed crew tries to log in:

Block access and show message:

"Your account has been removed. Please contact admin for assistance."

---

5. FILTER UPDATE

---

Add new tab in filters:

* Removed (0)

Now filters:
All | Pending | Approved | Rejected | Blacklisted | Removed

---

6. PAYMENT SAFETY CHECK

---

Before removing:

If payment is pending:
Show warning:

"This crew member has pending payments. Please clear payments before removing."

Option:

* Either block removal
  OR
* Allow override with confirmation

---

7. RE-ACTIVATE FEATURE (PRO)

---

Inside "Removed" tab:

* Replace actions with:
  View | Re-activate

Re-activate button:

* Changes status from "Removed" → "Approved"
* Crew can log in again

Confirmation popup:
"Re-activate this crew member?"

---

8. UI CONSISTENCY

---

* Match style with existing buttons (Reject, Blacklist)
* Maintain spacing, fonts, and hover effects
* Keep clean and minimal design

---

## FINAL RESULT

Admin flow:
Approve → Work → Blacklist → Pay → Remove → (Optional Re-activate)

Removed users:

* Cannot log in
* Not deleted
* Can be restored anytime

System remains safe, clean, and scalable.
